<p><p>Waterborne polyurethane resin for synthetic leather is used in two main processes: dry process and wet process. In the dry process, the resin is applied to the fabric by spray or roller coating, followed by drying and curing at room temperature. On the other hand, in the wet process, the fabric is immersed in a bath of the resin solution, followed by drying and curing using heat. Both processes have their advantages and are commonly used in the synthetic leather industry.</p></p>
