<p><p>3D hydrogels in cell culture can be classified into two main types: scaffold-free and scaffold-based. Scaffold-free hydrogels are matrices that allow cells to aggregate and interact with each other without the need for a physical support structure. On the other hand, scaffold-based hydrogels provide a framework for cells to attach and grow, mimicking the natural extracellular matrix. Both types have their unique advantages and applications in various cell culture models, offering researchers flexibility and versatility in their experiments.</p></p>
