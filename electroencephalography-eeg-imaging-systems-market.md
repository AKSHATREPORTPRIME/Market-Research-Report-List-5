<p><p>Electroencephalography (EEG) imaging systems are classified into three main types: Conventional EEG, Video EEG, and Dynamic EEG. Conventional EEG systems are used for routine EEG tests to diagnose epilepsy and sleep disorders. Video EEG systems combine video recording with EEG monitoring to capture behavioral changes during seizures. Dynamic EEG systems analyze brain activity in response to stimuli or tasks, providing insights into cognitive function. Each type of EEG system offers unique capabilities and applications in clinical settings.</p></p>
