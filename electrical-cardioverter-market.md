<p><p>The Electrical Cardioverter market can be divided into two main types: In Vivo and In Vitro. In Vivo devices are used inside the body to deliver electric shocks to restore normal heart rhythms, commonly known as implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs). In contrast, In Vitro devices are used outside the body to simulate and test electrical cardioversion procedures in laboratory settings. Both types play a crucial role in diagnosing and treating arrhythmias and other heart conditions, contributing to the overall growth of the market.</p></p>
