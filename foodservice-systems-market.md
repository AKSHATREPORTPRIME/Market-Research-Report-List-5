<p><ul><li>Conventional Foodservice System</li><li>Centralized Foodservice System</li><li>Ready-Prepared Foodservice System</li><li>Assembly Serve Foodservice System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Conventional Foodservice System involves preparing food on-site and serving it immediately to customers. Centralized Foodservice System prepares food at a central location and then distributes it to satellite locations for service. Ready-Prepared Foodservice System involves preparing and storing large quantities of food in advance, then reheating and serving it when needed. Assembly Serve Foodservice System requires minimal preparation, with pre-packaged or pre-portioned ingredients assembled and served to customers. These different market types cater to various foodservice needs and preferences.</p></p>
