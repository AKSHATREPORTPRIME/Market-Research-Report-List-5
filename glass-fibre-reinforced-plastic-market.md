<p><p>Glass Fibre Reinforced Plastic (GFRP) market incorporates two types of fibers: Continuous Fibers and Discontinuous (Short) Fibers. Continuous fibers are long strands that provide high strength and stiffness to the composite material, making it suitable for applications requiring structural integrity. Discontinuous fibers, on the other hand, are shorter strands that are dispersed randomly within the matrix, offering good impact resistance and cost-effectiveness. Both types of fibers contribute to the overall performance and characteristics of GFRP products in different ways.</p></p>
