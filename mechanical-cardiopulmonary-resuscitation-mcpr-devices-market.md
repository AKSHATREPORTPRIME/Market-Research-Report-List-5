<p><p>Mechanical Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (mCPR) devices are divided into two main types: electric and pneumatic. Electric mCPR devices rely on an electric motor to compress the chest during CPR, while pneumatic mCPR devices use compressed air to achieve chest compressions. Both types offer advantages such as consistent compression depth and rate, reducing fatigue for healthcare providers. The electric market is characterized by ease of use and portability, while the pneumatic market is known for its reliability and durability.</p></p>
