Acidic lubricating improvers are typically used in older diesel engines to help reduce wear and tear on engine components. Non-acidic lubricating improvers, on the other hand, are more commonly used in modern diesel engines as they are designed to offer improved lubrication without the potential corrosive effects of acidic additives.
