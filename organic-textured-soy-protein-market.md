<p><p>Organic textured soy protein comes in two main market types: granules and flour. Granules are small pieces of soy protein that have a meat-like texture and are commonly used as a meat substitute in dishes like chili and spaghetti. Flour, on the other hand, is finely ground soy protein that is used as a binding agent and thickening agent in various recipes such as veggie burgers and soups. Both granules and flour are popular choices for those looking to incorporate plant-based protein into their diet.</p></p>
